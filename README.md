# project-2

This project investigates the relationship between outing preferences, social behavior, and spending habits among young adults and university students. Through a survey-based approach, data was collected and analyzed to identify the most popular outing destinations, preferred times for social activities, and the factors that influence individuals when selecting places to visit. The study also examines how spending levels affect enjoyment and whether cost plays a significant role in overall outing satisfaction. By applying statistical analysis and data visualization techniques, the project highlights important behavioral patterns and trends within the target group. The results reveal that participants generally value atmosphere, comfort, and social interaction more than the actual cost of the outing, emphasizing the importance of experience over price.
